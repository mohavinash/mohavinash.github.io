# Bill No. 107 of 2026 — Complete Clause-by-Clause Reference

Source: Constitution (131st Amendment) Bill, 2026, as circulated to MPs on April 11, 2026.

---

## Clause 1 — Short Title and Commencement

- May be called the Constitution (One Hundred and Thirty-first Amendment) Act, 2026
- Comes into force on a date the Central Government notifies in the Official Gazette
- **Note:** The government controls when the amendment takes effect, not Parliament

---

## Clause 2 — Amendment of Article 55 (Presidential Election)

**Current:** "population" = last preceding census, construed as 1971 census until post-2026

**Proposed:** "population" = population as ascertained at such census, as Parliament may by law determine under article 82 or article 170

**Effect:** Presidential election vote weighting shifts from constitutional anchor (1971) to parliamentary discretion

---

## Clause 3 — Amendment of Article 81 (Lok Sabha Composition)

**Sub-clause (a):** Substitutes clause (1):
- **(a)** not more than **eight hundred and fifteen** members from States (was: 530)
- **(b)** not more than **thirty-five** members from UTs (was: 20)

**Sub-clause (b):** Substitutes clause (3):
- "population" = such census as Parliament may by law determine (was: last preceding census, construed as 1971/2001)

**Effect:** Lok Sabha cap raised from 550 to 850. Census choice delegated to ordinary law.

---

## Clause 4 — Amendment of Article 82 (Delimitation Trigger)

**Sub-clause (a):** Marginal heading changed from "Readjustment after each census" to "Readjustment of constituencies"
- **Significance:** Removes the automatic link between census completion and delimitation

**Sub-clause (b):** "Upon the completion of each census, the allocation of seats" → "The allocation of seats"
- **Significance:** Delimitation is no longer triggered by census completion

**Sub-clause (c):** "by such authority and in such manner" → "in such manner and on the basis of such census, by the Delimitation Commission"
- **Significance:** Names the Delimitation Commission explicitly in the Constitution (previously only in ordinary legislation)

**Sub-clause (d):** **The third proviso shall be omitted.**
- **This is the key change.** The third proviso (inserted by 84th Amendment, 2001) is what froze seat allocation until post-2026 census. Deleting it removes the freeze entirely.

---

## Clause 5 — Amendment of Article 170 (State Assemblies)

Mirrors Clause 4 exactly but for state legislative assemblies:

**Sub-clause (a):** Removes "Subject to the provisions of article 333"

**Sub-clause (b):** "population" = such census as Parliament may by law determine (was: 2001 census)

**Sub-clause (c)(i):** Removes "Upon the completion of each census" trigger

**Sub-clause (c)(ii):** Names the Delimitation Commission explicitly

**Sub-clause (c)(iii):** **Third proviso omitted** — removes the freeze on state assembly seats

---

## Clause 6 — Amendment of Article 330 (SC/ST Reservation in Lok Sabha)

Substitutes the Explanation after clause (3):

"population" = such census as Parliament may by law determine (was: 2001 census, frozen until post-2026)

**Effect:** SC/ST reservation seat calculations shift to whatever census Parliament chooses

---

## Clause 7 — Amendment of Article 332 (SC/ST Reservation in State Assemblies)

Substitutes clauses (3A) and (3B) with updated language for:
- **Arunachal Pradesh, Meghalaya, Mizoram, Nagaland** (3A): Upon readjustment under Art. 170, ST seats shall be proportional to existing Assembly composition (preserves NE tribal representation)
- **Tripura** (3B): Same protection for ST seats

**Note:** These are **protective clauses** ensuring NE states with high tribal populations don't lose ST representation during delimitation. The bill preserves these protections.

---

## Clause 8 — Substitution of Article 334A (Women's Reservation)

Replaces the entire Article 334A (inserted by 106th Amendment, 2023):

**334A(1):** Women's reservation takes effect "after an exercise of delimitation is undertaken for this purpose"
- **Key change:** Original 334A required delimitation based on "first census taken after commencement" (i.e., post-2023 census). New text removes this — any delimitation exercise suffices.

**334A(2):** Reservation expires **15 years from commencement of the 106th Amendment (2023)** unless Parliament extends by law.
- Expiry date: ~September 2038
- Extension requires ordinary legislation

**334A(3):** Reserved seats allotted by rotation to different constituencies after each delimitation.

**334A(4):** Current representation not affected until dissolution of existing House.

**Key additions vs original 334A:**
- Now covers **Puducherry** and **Jammu & Kashmir** Legislative Assemblies (not just Delhi NCT)
- Removes the census-trigger requirement
- Clarifies that rotation happens after each subsequent delimitation

---

## Statement of Objects and Reasons — Key Paragraphs

**Para 4:** "the country's demographic profile has since undergone substantial changes... including significant inter-State and intra-State population shifts, rapid urbanisation and migration, and disproportionate growth in certain regions, resulting in wide disparities in the population and the constituencies"

**Para 6:** "The next census and the consequential delimitation exercise thereafter will take considerable time and thus, delay the effective and dedicated participation of women in our democratic polity... the implementation of reservation of seats for women is linked to the constitutional scheme of readjustment in the allocation of seats"

**Para 7:** "The proposed Bill would facilitate delimitation of territorial constituencies and put in operation, the provisions providing for reservation of seats for women... This will also promote women empowerment and provide opportunity for women to participate in the nation-building process."

**Sponsor:** Arjun Ram Meghwal, Minister of State (I/C) for Law & Justice and Minister of State of Parliamentary Affairs. Dated April 11, 2026.
