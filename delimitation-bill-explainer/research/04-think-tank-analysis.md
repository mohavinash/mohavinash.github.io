# Think Tank Analysis and Expert Commentary

## Key Sources

### 1. IJRISS — North-South Divide: Constitutional Challenges
- **URL:** [rsisinternational.org/journals/ijriss/articles/north-south-divide-constitutional-challenges-and-federal-implications-of-indias-2026-delimitation-exercise/](https://rsisinternational.org/journals/ijriss/articles/north-south-divide-constitutional-challenges-and-federal-implications-of-indias-2026-delimitation-exercise/)
- **Key finding:** Population projections indicate northern states potentially gaining 43 parliamentary seats while southern states lose 24 in relative terms.

### 2. The Squirrels — Demographic Penalty on Southern India
- **URL:** [thesquirrels.in/politics/india-2026-delimitation-demographic-penalty-south-11257598](https://thesquirrels.in/politics/india-2026-delimitation-demographic-penalty-south-11257598)
- **Key finding:** Between 2001 and 2026, UP+Uttarakhand population grew ~55.33%, Tamil Nadu only 15.5%. Southern states face "severe relative reduction" in representation.

### 3. The Squirrels — 816-Seat Lok Sabha Expansion
- **URL:** [thesquirrels.in/politics/lok-sabha-816-seats-delimitation-north-south-divide-11261651](https://thesquirrels.in/politics/lok-sabha-816-seats-delimitation-north-south-divide-11261651)
- **Key finding:** Under 816-seat model, UP could surge to 120+ seats. Even maintaining proportions while expanding total seats still dilutes southern political power due to wider absolute gaps.

### 4. NUS ISAS — Delimitation: Southern States Up in Arms
- **URL:** [isas.nus.edu.sg/papers/delimitation-of-constituencies-in-india-southern-states-up-in-arms/](https://www.isas.nus.edu.sg/papers/delimitation-of-constituencies-in-india-southern-states-up-in-arms/)
- **Key finding:** Academic analysis of the federal implications of population-based reapportionment.

### 5. India House Foundation — Delimitation Dilemma
- **URL:** [indiahousefoundation.org/delimitation-dilemma-will-the-south-lose-its-voice-in-indias-next-political-map](https://indiahousefoundation.org/delimitation-dilemma-will-the-south-lose-its-voice-in-indias-next-political-map)
- **Key finding:** States that succeeded in population stabilization are "penalized by holding fewer seats relative to their population share."

### 6. Drishti IAS — Balancing the North-South Divide
- **URL:** [drishtiias.com/daily-updates/daily-news-analysis/balancing-the-north-south-divide-in-delimitation](https://www.drishtiias.com/daily-updates/daily-news-analysis/balancing-the-north-south-divide-in-delimitation)
- **Key finding:** Proposes weighted formula: 70–80% population + 20–30% development indicators (literacy, health, fertility control).

### 7. ForumIAS — Delimitation After 2027
- **URL:** [forumias.com/blog/delimitation-after-2027-redrawing-power-in-india/](https://forumias.com/blog/delimitation-after-2027-redrawing-power-in-india/)
- **Key finding:** Proposes "Federal Representation Index" incorporating population, literacy, healthcare, and economic output.

### 8. Rohan Venkat — India's Delimitation Dilemma (Substack)
- **URL:** [rohanvenkat.substack.com/p/how-will-india-solve-its-delimitation](https://rohanvenkat.substack.com/p/how-will-india-solve-its-delimitation)
- **Key finding:** Analysis of the political economy of delimitation.

## Alternative Formula Proposals

Several think tanks have proposed compromise formulas to balance population representation with federal equity:

| Proposal | Formula | Source |
|----------|---------|--------|
| Weighted formula | 70–80% population + 20–30% development indicators | Drishti IAS, ForumIAS |
| Federal Representation Index | Composite of population, literacy, healthcare, economic output | ForumIAS |
| DemPer principle | Blend population, TFR, and HDI | Various academic proposals |
| Decouple approach | Implement women's reservation within existing 543 seats, separate from delimitation | Opposition parties |

## Political Reactions

### Opposition
- **Sonia Gandhi** (Congress): Objected to timing, described rejection of all-party meeting as "one-upmanship." Congress supports women's reservation but "objects to the delimitation process being carried out before the 2027 Census." ([The Week, April 13, 2026](https://www.theweek.in/news/india/2026/04/13/what-strategy-will-congress-adopt-on-womens-reservation-and-delimitation-bills.html))
- Congress issued a three-line whip mandating MP attendance during the April 16–18 special session.

### Government
- The bill is positioned as enabling women's empowerment and "Viksit Bharat@2047."
- Reports indicate the plan is 816 seats (543 existing + 273 new women-reserved constituencies), preserving all male incumbents. ([Times of India, March 24, 2026](https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/india/parliament-seats-may-be-increased-to-816-for-1/3rd-womens-reservation/articleshow/129764257.cms))
