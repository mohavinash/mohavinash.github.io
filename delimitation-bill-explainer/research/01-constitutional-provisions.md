# Constitutional Provisions on Delimitation and Seat Allocation

## Article 81 — Composition of the House of the People

**Current text (as amended by 84th & 87th Amendments):** The Lok Sabha shall consist of not more than 530 members chosen by direct election from territorial constituencies in the States, and not more than 20 members to represent the Union Territories.

- "Population" means the population as ascertained at the last preceding census — but with a proviso (84th Amendment, 2001): until the first census taken after the year 2026, "population" means the 1971 census for total seat allocation.
- The 87th Amendment (2003) clarified that for internal constituency boundary redrawing (not total seat count), the 2001 census figures are used.

**Key effect:** Total Lok Sabha seats per state have been frozen based on 1971 population figures since 1976. Only internal constituency boundaries were redrawn using 2001 census data.

**Source:** Constitution of India, Article 81; [84th Amendment Act, 2001](https://www.eci.gov.in/delimitation); [87th Amendment Act, 2003](https://legislative.gov.in/constitution-eighty-seventh-amendment-act-2003)

---

## Article 82 — Readjustment After Each Census

**Current text:** Upon completion of each census, the allocation of seats in the Lok Sabha to the states and the division of each state into territorial constituencies shall be readjusted by such authority and in such manner as Parliament may by law determine.

- **First proviso:** Readjustment shall not affect representation until dissolution of the then-existing House.
- **Second proviso:** Readjustment takes effect from such date as the President may specify.
- **Third proviso (84th Amendment):** Until the first census taken after 2026, it shall not be necessary to readjust total seat allocation or territorial division.

**Significance of the third proviso:** This is the legal mechanism of the "seat freeze." Removing it unlocks delimitation.

**Source:** Constitution of India, Article 82

---

## Article 170 — Composition of State Legislative Assemblies

Each state assembly shall have not less than 60 and not more than 500 members. Mirrors the same freeze as Article 82:
- 84th Amendment: population means 1971 census for total seats
- 87th Amendment: 2001 census for internal boundaries
- Third proviso freezes total assembly seats until post-2026

**Source:** Constitution of India, Article 170

---

## Article 55 — Manner of Election of the President

The President is elected by an electoral college. Each MLA's vote = (state population / total MLAs) / 1000. "Population" was also frozen at 1971 census by the 84th Amendment.

**Why this matters:** If Lok Sabha seats are reallocated by current population but Article 55 still uses 1971 figures, there would be an inconsistency.

**Source:** Constitution of India, Article 55

---

## Articles 330 and 332 — SC/ST Reservation

Article 330 reserves Lok Sabha seats for SC/ST in proportion to their population. Article 332 does the same for state assemblies. The 84th Amendment froze total reserved seats at 1971 levels; the 87th Amendment allowed specific reserved constituencies to be redrawn using 2001 data.

**Source:** Constitution of India, Articles 330, 332

---

## Article 334A — Women's Reservation (106th Amendment, 2023)

Inserted by the Nari Shakti Vandan Adhiniyam. Provides for reservation of one-third of seats for women in Lok Sabha and state assemblies.

**Critical condition:** Reservation takes effect only after a delimitation exercise based on the first census taken after commencement of this amendment (i.e., after 2023). This tied women's reservation to the next delimitation.

**Source:** [PRS Legislative Research — Women's Reservation Bill 2023](https://prsindia.org/billtrack/the-constitution-one-hundred-twenty-eighth-amendment-bill-2023); [106th Amendment, Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/One_Hundred_and_Sixth_Amendment_of_the_Constitution_of_India)

---

## Article 368 — The Amendment Process

Constitutional amendments require:
- **Special majority:** Majority of total membership AND two-thirds majority of members present and voting, in each House.
- **State ratification (for federal matters):** Amendments affecting representation of states in Parliament require ratification by at least half of state legislatures (Article 368(2) proviso).

**Delimitation-related amendments require state ratification** because they alter seat allocation to states.

**Source:** Constitution of India, Article 368

---

## The 131st Amendment Bill (2026) — Proposed Changes

**Key change 1:** Replaces "the last preceding census" with "such census as Parliament may by law determine" in Articles 55, 81, 82, 170, 330, and 332. This shifts the census choice from constitutional mandate to ordinary legislation (simple majority, no state ratification needed for future changes).

**Key change 2:** Deletes the third proviso in Articles 82 and 170, removing the 2026 freeze entirely.

**Key change 3:** Raises Lok Sabha cap from 550 to 850 (815 states + 35 UTs).

**Key change 4:** Substitutes Article 334A to allow delimitation on the basis of any census, not just the first census after 2023.

**Status:** Bill circulated to MPs on April 11, 2026. Scheduled for introduction in Lok Sabha on April 16, 2026 during a special session.
