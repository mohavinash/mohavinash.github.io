### 3. Bill Status — FIXED
- **Claim:** "Introduced April 11, 2026"
- **Correct:** Bill was **circulated** to MPs on April 11. Formal introduction scheduled for **April 16, 2026** during a special session.
- **Fixed in:** `index.html`, `bill.html`, `sources.html`, `clauses.html`, `data.js`

### 4. Census Status — FIXED
- **Claim:** "2021 census postponed indefinitely"

- **Fixed in:** `data.js` (TIMELINE)

### 5. 42nd Amendment Wording — FIXED
- **Claim:** "freezes seat allocation until 2001"
- **Correct:** "freezes seat allocation until the first census after **2000**" (the 2001 census was the first after 2000)
- **Fixed in:** `data.js` (TIMELINE)

## KEY HISTORICAL FINDING

The 84th Amendment (2001) directly substituted "2000" with "2026." The chain is:
1. 42nd Amendment (1976): "until first census after year **2000**"
2. 84th Amendment (2001): substituted "2000" → "**2026**"

There was no intermediate "after 2001" freeze. COVID did not cause any constitutional amendment to extend the freeze. The freeze to "after 2026" has been in place since 2001.
